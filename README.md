This paper addresses the critical challenge of accurate and efficient brain tumor detection from Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) scans, a task often hindered by time-consuming manual interpretation, inter-observer variability, noise and low tissue contrast. This study systematically compares the performance of classical ML models, including SVM, Random Forests, XGBoost, Decison Tree and K-NN, with CNN and MobileNetV2 architectures for brain tumor classification.
